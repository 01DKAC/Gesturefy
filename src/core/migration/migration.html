Gesturefy Major Update 3.0

Gesturefy has been Upgraded to version 3.0. Please take a few minutes to read about the changes.

This update changes the core functionallity of Gesturefy. A gesture is no longer a sequence of the 4 directions up, down, right and left. Instead Gesturefy does now record the entire mouse movement as a sequence of vectors.

This provides three main benefits:

  • You can now define and use much more complex gestures. Whereas previously you were limited to the 4 directions you can now use any mouse movement as a gesture. So diagonal or circular gestures are no longer a problem. This also enables the use of symbolic gestures like letters, symbols, or even the crossed house puzzle.

  • The gesture representation is more comprehensible and natural, which also adds up to a better gesture overview. You no longer have to read the directions of each gesture and transform them in your head to an actual mouse movement. Instead you immediately see how you have to move your mouse. The resulting gesture path is also easier to remember than a combination of certain directions.

  • Previously tiny mouse movements could lead to an undesired combination of directions and thus to no or wrong command execution. The new matching algorithm includes a deviation tolerance, so you don't need to draw as accurate as before. This value can also be adjusted under settings > advanced options. Be aware: If you tend to cancel your gestures by randomly moving your mouse, a gesture might still match due to the deviation tolerance.

What about disadvantages?

The migration of your gestures to the new gesture format cannot be fully automated. This is because the conversion is not bijective and depends on your personal gesture style. Because of that you should readjust your gestures that consisted of more than 1 direction. Otherwise some of your gestures might not work as expected.

Additional noteworthy changes:

  • Added a browser action/icon to allow new users to quickly open Gesturefy's option page. This can be removed by right clicking the icon and choosing "remove from toolbar"
  • Gesturefy is now working with privacy.resistFingerprinting = true
  • You can define the position of the gesture/command name
  • More commands, settings and fixes. For more information read the full changelog.
